# Parkinson’s Disease Place of Death Analysis (PDD_systreview)

This repository contains the code and documentation for teh systematic review and meta-analysis investigating factors associated with the place of death in individuals with Parkinson’s disease.

## Introduction

Parkinson’s disease is linked to increased mortality, and hospitalizations at the end of life are common among affected individuals. Despite this, there is limited evidence regarding the factors that influence the place of death in this population.

## Objectives

The primary objective of this work is to explore factors associated with the place of death of individuals with Parkinson’s disease.

## Methods

- **Data Sources:**  
  Three electronic databases (MEDLINE, EMBASE, and PsycINFO) were searched for studies investigating the place of death among individuals with Parkinson’s disease. No time or language limitations were applied.

- **Analysis Approach:**  
  Where possible, a meta-analysis of factors was performed using a random-effects model. A meta-regression model was also utilized to adjust for country-specific availability of long-term care beds. Results were expressed as odds ratios (OR) for the place of death. Sensitivity analyses were conducted to explore heterogeneity.

## Conclusions

This systematic review and meta-analysis identify key factors associated with hospital death in individuals with Parkinson’s disease. The findings can inform clinical decision-making and policy planning, support the development of targeted screening interventions, and help allocate resources more effectively. Further research is recommended to explore additional influencing factors across various care settings.

## Key Message

- **Strong Evidence:**  
  The study identifies key factors influencing the place of death in Parkinson’s disease. Strong evidence links male gender, advanced age, and marital status to increased hospital deaths.
